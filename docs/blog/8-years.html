8 Years - A Reflection

It's currently 5:43 PM on Wednesday March 22nd 2023. About 15 minutes ago I decided to take a break from what I was working on, and from within a crevice in my mind, a memory suddenly arose. The memory was of the days I would spend back in the sixth grade on my school-assigned chromebook, exploring a little internet world called Scratch.

I thought of the hours and hours I spent back then, building these funny little games by dragging and dropping blocks and connecting them to each other. Drawing characters that I could move around, recording myself making sound effects, and recording music from YouTube to serve as background music (yes, I would actually record YouTube videos on my laptop's microphone).

Then I would share the projects for the rest of the scratchers to see, and hoped that all of my effort would be recognized and appreciated. I'd stay up late at night working on these games and then go to sleep praying that they would miraculously blow up over night. And I think I built up a good collection of online friends in this space, too.

One of the games I made, "Ninja Bounce", got popular in my school. I think one of my friends started playing it, and showed it to his friends, and they started playing it too. It was a really simple little game where a "ninja" bounced on the ground while trying to not to touch a "robot" that moved around and sped up with every bounce. Every time the ninja bounced, the score would go up. There was also a "world-score" feature which used cloud data.

I think the simplicity and "choppiness" of the game is what made it fun. It was the project I was most proud of. Not because I spent a lot of time on it (in fact, I barely spent any time), but because there was a kid at my school who was shocked to hear that I was the one who made it, because I guess I was unpopular or something.

Does Scratch even exist anymore? Turns out it does. I just logged into it, and the nostalgia hit like a metal pipe. It's probably been 6 years since I last logged in. Everything is still there. Everything looks the same (except the editor, which they horizontally flipped for some reason). People still play my games, and remix my projects, and leave nice comments on my profile.

What stood out to me the most was the text at the top of my profile: "Joined 8 years, 1 month ago". Honestly I wasn't really surprised at the size of that number. What interested me more was how involving myself in this mini-world on the internet 8 years ago led to my current circumstance. If it wasn't for Scratch, I would've never been interested in coding. That's what sparked my love for creation, and art, and computers. I probably wouldn't have even considered Computer Science as my major in university if it wasn't for it.

Scratch literally changed my life forever. It's a domino effect. My middle school teacher introduced me to it, then I explored a bit on my own and got hooked. Imagine if I never did!

And this whole experience has me wondering, where will I be 8 years from now? What accomplishments will I have made? What would be my role in society? And how will my experiences today effect that outcome?
